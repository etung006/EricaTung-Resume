After graduating high school, I decided to further my academic goals at the University of California, Riverside. As a first year, some of the things I've learned is that your time is much better spent involved with school activities. I was shy and nervous coming into such a big school, but once I started exploring what they had to offer, I became more comfortable and confident.

Although the stigma around greek life seems to revolve around not focusing on your studies, my experience has been the exact opposite. The main organization I'm in currently is Gamma Phi Beta. Ever since joining, I've met a lot of my closest friends through recruitment and have been actively involved in school and sorority events. I regularly attend our bi-weekly business meetings that have helped prepare me for future skills I may need in the future. Some skills I've inherited are time-management, budgeting, and presenting in front of an audience.

School has always been something that came more naturally to me. I never really struggled in highschool until COVID hit and the online instruction distracted me easily as I wasn't in a classroom environment, but the comfort of my own home. In the three quarters I've attended UCR, I can say that having in-person classes are a sure way to succeed to in school. Staying on top of your school work and balance deadlines with wanting a social life is not easy, but it can be done. Prioritizing yourself and figuring out a routine that works for you is the most crucial thing you can do.
